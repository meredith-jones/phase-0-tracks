MJ's top 5 WA hikes!!!

...in no particular order...

  1. Spray Park, Mount Rainier National Park
    • On a clear day, this hike features epic views of the Mt. Rainier
    • The wildflowers are to die for
    • There are a couple really pretty stream crossings
    • You can take a quick side path to Spray Falls, which is also incredible!

  2. Hoh River Trail, Olympic National Park
    • This is the PNW's very own rainforest
    • It is incredibly green and the air is very clean
    • You're bound to see cool critters and interesting plants
    • It is along a river and features stream crossings and waterfalls.

  3. Mt. Ellinor, Olympic Peninsula
    • Short and sweet hike with amazing 360 views! Very steep though-- be careful.
    • You might see mountain goats!
    • On a clear day, you can see Mt. Rainier.
    • It features a variety of terrain.

  4. Blanca Lake, North Cascades
    • A bit of a difficult hike, but the views along the way are gorgeous.
    • Nothing compares to the view of the lake itself--it's color is unlike any other.
    • You can jump in the lake! It's very ... refreshing.

  5. Second Beach, Olympic Peninsula
    • Quick and easy hike with a great reward--the views at the beach are awesome!
    • Very interesting rock formations on the coast
    • Really nice forest trail, well maintained.
    • You'll see very interesting plant life!

For more info on these and other Washington hikes, visit wta.org.
